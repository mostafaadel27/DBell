## Design Context

### Users
Men and women aged 18–40 who are serious about fitness and willing to pay for quality. They range from beginners to advanced. Their mindset is focused on real physical transformation. They value results and the experience, avoiding the cheapest options.

### Brand Personality
**Strong, Premium, Motivational**  
DBell is a hybrid of a hardcore performance gym and a high-end luxury experience. It should evoke feelings of strength 💪, motivation 🔥, confidence 🧠, and exclusivity 👑. It is a serious, results-driven environment with a luxury edge—not a casual neighborhood gym or a calm wellness spa.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Premium + Aggressive** (High-contrast, clean & intentional spacing)  
Strictly **dark mode**. The color palette is restricted to black, red, and white. The aesthetic combines the minimalism and strength of Nike Training, the exclusivity of Equinox, and the energy of Gymshark. 

**Anti-references**: Overly colorful or chaotic layouts, cheap/outdated designs, and excessive or distracting animations. 

### Design Principles
1. **Premium Utility**: Use minimal, high-impact UI focused on powerful real training imagery. Do not use generic 3-column benefit cards or generic AI slop metric templates.
2. **Bold Hierarchy**: Rely heavily on strong, bold typography (not overused defaults) and raw, intentional asymmetry to guide focus.
3. **Intentional Contrast**: Master high contrast interactions using primarily black, white, and intense red accents to exude strength.
4. **Purposeful Motion**: Keep motion sharp, minimal, and purposeful. Strictly avoid spring/bounce easing—animations should be quick and natural (e.g., expo/quart). Respect reduced motion preferences.
5. **Accessible Strength**: Ensure robust accessibility (WCAG AA) with clear contrast and interactive semantics (using buttons/links, not divs), proving that dark/aggressive UI doesn't sacrifice usability.
